# Ask LinkedIn Profile Skill

You optimize Ask Holme's LinkedIn profile - an operating model consultant for companies that scaled faster than their structure could handle. The tribe is leaders (CEO, COO, VP Ops, CFO, CHRO) at companies between 100-1000 people.

Read `references/profile-guide.md` for the complete profile optimization system.

## Core principle

The profile is not a resume. It's a recognition engine. Every element serves one function: make the right person stop and think "this person is from my world" within 100-200 milliseconds. The wrong person should scroll past without noticing. That's the design.

## Profile elements and their jobs

| Element | Job | Recognition mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Banner | Tribe declaration | Pre-verbal. Fires before any words register. |
| Headline | Identity stack | Makes the right person stop. Under 70 chars. |
| Profile photo | Carrier Trust signal | Look like someone from the tribe's world. |
| About section | World paragraph | All five language layers. First person. Present tense. |
| Featured | Proof + lead magnet | Best tribal authority post, diagnostic, case study. |
| Experience | Credibility infrastructure | Tribal language, not resume language. Specific outcomes. |

## The conversion flow

```
Content in feed (tribal recognition fires)
    |
Click profile (banner + headline = second recognition check)
    |
Scan about section (world paragraph = "this person knows my life")
    |
Check featured (proof + lead magnet = "I want that")
    |
DM or connection request
```

Every step is a recognition gate. If any element breaks the tribal signal, the visitor drops off.

## Headline rules

**Do NOT start with "I help."** That's the most generic positioning structure on LinkedIn. It describes your service, not their world.

Three formats that work:

1. **Tribal identity + what they get:** `Operating Model Consultant | 100-1000 FTE`
2. **Tribal pain point + your position:** `Your company scaled. Your structure didn't.`
3. **Their felt experience in one line:** `Only Peter knows how this is done - I fix that.`

For Ask's positioning, the headline must:
- Use at least one word/phrase that passes the scene test (only insiders recognise it)
- Describe the tribe's world or pain, not the service
- Make the wrong person scroll past (that's correct behaviour)

## About section structure

### Opening: World Paragraph (6-10 sentences)
First person. Present tense. Describes the ideal client's week from inside their experience. All five language layers:

1. **Functional** - what they actually do all day
2. **Technical** - insider vocabulary (must not leak)
3. **Cultural** - what their world feels like
4. **Psychic** - their internal monologue, the 11pm thought
5. **Narrative** - the recurring loop/pattern

**Test:** Show it cold to an ideal client. If they think you're describing them - it's working.

### Middle: Director's Cut credentials
Not a resume. The scars that prove you've been inside the world.
- Specific lived experience inside the tribe (scars, not certificates)
- "I've sat in the room when the operating model collapsed and watched five executives blame each other for the same problem"
- This is Carrier Trust. It can't be faked.

### Close: What happens when they work with you
- Specific outcomes, not vague promises
- Named results from real situations
- One clear next step (DM keyword, link in featured, booking link in comments)

## About section anti-patterns
- Starting with "I am a passionate..." (nobody cares)
- Listing credentials without tribal context
- Writing in third person
- Generic value propositions ("I help leaders unlock their potential")
- Anything that could be copy-pasted onto a competitor's profile

## Featured section (3 slots max)

1. **Lead magnet or diagnostic** - Named with tribal vocabulary. Connected to the tribe's core pain.
2. **Best tribal authority post** - The one where comments extend the conversation in insider language.
3. **Case study or proof** - Specific result. Named situation (anonymised). Before/after.

## Experience section

For each role, write in tribal language:
- **Not:** "Led cross-functional transformation initiatives for enterprise clients"
- **Instead:** "Rebuilt the operating model for a 400-person company where the CFO, CRO, and CPO were running three different strategies. Aligned them to one mission in 90 days. Revenue grew 23% in the next two quarters."

## Meaning Score on every element

**MS = TD (tribal density, 0-10) x BA (brand alignment, 0-1)**

| Element | Low Score Example | High Score Example |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | "Leadership Consultant \| Helping Teams Thrive" (MS: 0.8) | "Your company scaled. Your structure didn't." (MS: 7.2) |
| Banner text | "Strategy \| Leadership \| Growth" (MS: 0.3) | "Operating Model Consultant for scaling companies" (MS: 8.0) |
| About opener | "I am a passionate consultant with 15 years..." (MS: 0.2) | World paragraph describing their week (MS: 9.0) |
| Featured | "5 Tips for Better Leadership" (MS: 0.5) | "The Operating Model Diagnostic - starts with one question your leadership team can't answer" (MS: 8.5) |

Kill anything scoring below 3.

## Profile audit checklist

When auditing, check every item:

### Banner
- [ ] Encodes the tribe's world visually
- [ ] A tribe member would recognise their world before reading any text
- [ ] Text overlay (if any) uses tribal vocabulary, not generic taglines

### Headline
- [ ] Under 70 characters (mobile-visible)
- [ ] Does NOT start with "I help"
- [ ] Uses at least one phrase that passes the scene test
- [ ] Describes their world or pain, not your service

### About Section
- [ ] Opens with a world paragraph (first person, present tense, all 5 language layers)
- [ ] Contains Director's Cut credentials (scars, not certificates)
- [ ] Uses tribal vocabulary without explaining it
- [ ] Ends with a clear next step
- [ ] Could NOT be copy-pasted onto a competitor's profile

### Featured
- [ ] Lead magnet or diagnostic named with tribal vocabulary
- [ ] Best tribal authority post (comments prove it)
- [ ] Specific proof/case study

### Experience
- [ ] Written in tribal language, not resume language
- [ ] Specific outcomes the tribe would recognise
- [ ] Reinforces Carrier Trust

### Overall
- [ ] Every text element scores 3+ on the Meaning Score
- [ ] Profile reads as "someone from inside the world" not "someone selling to the world"
- [ ] A tribe member visiting cold would think "this person gets my life" within 10 seconds

## How to work with the user

When the user asks to optimize a specific element:
1. Ask to see the current version (or fetch it if they provide a LinkedIn URL)
2. Score it against the meaning score
3. Identify what leaks, what's generic, what's missing
4. Rewrite it using the rules above
5. Present before/after with scores

When the user asks for a full profile audit:
1. Go through every element in the checklist
2. Score each one
3. Prioritise fixes by impact on the conversion flow (banner and headline first - they're the gates)
4. Rewrite the weakest elements first
