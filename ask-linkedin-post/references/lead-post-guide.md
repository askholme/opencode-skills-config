# Guide: Writing a LinkedIn Post That Generates Leads

## The Non-Negotiable Before You Write

Your post must land in the **Tribal Authority** quadrant - high tribal density AND high brand alignment simultaneously. If either dimension is zero, the post produces zero leads.

**Pre-flight check:**
- Does this post use scenes only my tribe would recognise? (Tribal Density)
- Does this post position me as the person who solves their specific problem? (Brand Alignment)
- Would Steven Bartlett never write this? (Specificity test)

If any answer is no, rewrite before publishing.

---

## Post Anatomy: The Movie Trailer Framework

Every lead-generating post needs four components in the hook (first 2-3 lines before "see more"):

### 1. Cast, Setting, Genre
Named things the tribe already knows. People, tools, roles, situations. This is the familiarity layer - it tells the reader "this is your world" before they've consciously assessed anything.

- **Good:** "I asked one CEO to poll his six direct reports..."
- **Bad:** "Leaders should align on strategy..."

### 2. Story
Implied payoff. The reader needs to know what they'll get if they click "see more." A lesson, a breakdown, a reframe, a diagnostic.

### 3. Conflict
Contradiction or tension that creates cognitive friction. Two things that shouldn't coexist. The reader's brain can't resolve it without reading further.

- **Good:** "He got six different answers. That was the diagnosis."
- **Bad:** "Alignment is important for organisations."

### 4. Director's Cut
YOUR perspective from YOUR lived experience. The irreplaceable human layer. Not aggregated advice. Not something AI could produce from a generic prompt. Your opinion, earned from being in the room.

---

## Content Depth: Minimum Level 4

Posts below Level 4 don't generate leads. Period.

| Level | What It Sounds Like | Leads? |
|---|---|---|
| 1: Label | "I help companies with their operating model." | No |
| 2: Stacked Labels | "I help mid-market companies fix cross-functional alignment." | No |
| 3: World Description | "Companies with 200-500 people often struggle when each function optimises for its own metric." | No |
| **4: Tribal Content** | "The CFO optimises for margin. The CRO optimises for revenue. The CPO optimises for product innovation. All rational. All defensible. All pulling in different directions." | **Yes** |
| **5: Director's Cut** | Same as 4, plus your opinion from lived experience fixing this exact problem. Named situations. Specific outcomes. | **Yes** |

**The Kitchen Counter rule:** Level 4 names specific things happening in the tribe's world RIGHT NOW. Not timeless generic advice. Current + deep simultaneously.

---

## The Five Language Layers in a Single Post

A lead-generating post doesn't need all five in every post, but the best ones hit 3-4:

1. **Functional** - What they actually do. "Poll his six direct reports." "Set Q2 priorities."
2. **Technical** - Insider vocabulary. "OKRs." "Strategic pillars." "Operating model."
3. **Cultural** - What their world feels like. "A slide deck with six strategic pillars." "Town halls." "Strategy offsites."
4. **Psychic** - Their internal monologue. The thing they think at 11pm but never say in the boardroom.
5. **Narrative** - The recurring loop. "Every quarter they realign. Every quarter it drifts again."

## Post Structure That Converts

### Hook (lines 1-3)
- One idea. Maximum tension. Minimum words.
- Pattern interrupt, story loop, bold claim, or contrarian reframe.
- Must earn the "see more" click.

### Body
- One idea per paragraph.
- Short paragraphs: 1-2 sentences each.
- Line breaks between every paragraph. White space = readability.
- Numbered lists for sequential content.
- Use tribal vocabulary without explaining it. If you explain it, you're writing for outsiders.

### Close
- Restate the core insight in one sentence.
- Make it actionable or diagnostic ("ask yourself this question").

### CTA
- DM CTA for lead generation: "DM me '[KEYWORD]' and I'll send you [specific resource]."
- Question CTA for engagement: Ask something the tribe has a strong opinion on.
- Never put external links in the post body. Comments only.

## The Meaning Score Check

Before publishing, score every key phrase:

**MS = TD x BA**

- TD (Tribal Density): 0-10. Does this word mean something specific to my tribe?
- BA (Brand Alignment): 0-1. Does this word position me as the solver?

Kill every phrase scoring below 3.

## Three Lead Post Archetypes

### 1. The Diagnostic Post
Name the problem the tribe can't see. Give them a test to run. Offer the deeper diagnostic via DM.

**Structure:** Observation -> Specific symptoms -> The reframe ("you don't have an X problem, you have a Y problem") -> One-question test -> DM CTA.

### 2. The Kitchen Counter Post
React to something happening in the tribe's world right now. Policy change, industry event, common failure pattern you're seeing this quarter.

**Structure:** Current event/observation -> Why it matters (tribal interpretation) -> Your Director's Cut opinion -> Implication for the reader -> CTA.

### 3. The Celebrity Contrast Post
Reference a known figure or brand the tribe has opinions about. Borrow their recognition, redirect to your positioning.

**Structure:** Famous example -> Contradiction or contrast -> Your accessible alternative -> Proof/specifics -> CTA.

## Algorithm Signals That Matter (2026)

Ranked by weight:

1. **Dwell time** - How long people stop scrolling to read. Tribal density drives this.
2. **Meaningful comments** (5+ words) - Most valuable engagement signal.
3. **Saves/bookmarks** - High-value signal.
4. **Shares** - Especially to feed.
5. **Reactions** - Least weighted.

**What kills reach:**
- External links in post body
- Editing within first hour
- Posting more than once per day
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree")
- Tagging people who don't engage back

**What amplifies reach:**
- Author commenting within first hour
- Replying to every comment in first 2 hours
- Comments that extend the conversation in insider language (Level 4+ indicator)

---

## Posting Cadence

- **Optimal times:** Tue-Thu 7:30-8:30am, 12:00-1:00pm (audience timezone)
- **Frequency:** 3-5 posts/week for growth. Minimum 2 for maintaining reach.
- **Format ranking:** Text-only (long) or text + selfie photo perform best for authority content. Carousels for frameworks/step-by-step.

---

## The Comment Diagnostic

After publishing, read your comments. They tell you exactly what level your post hit.

| Comment Type | Your Content Level |
|---|---|
| "Great post!" + fire emoji | Level 1-3. Category content. |
| "This resonates" (no specifics) | Level 3. Warm but generic. |
| Comments using insider language, extending the conversation, adding their own tribal examples | Level 4-5. Tribal Authority. |
| DMs saying "how do you know my life?" | Level 5. Director's Cut. |

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- [ ] Hook earns the "see more" click (conflict or story loop in first 2 lines)
- [ ] Uses tribal vocabulary without explaining it
- [ ] Names specific things (Kitchen Counter items), not generic categories
- [ ] Contains your Director's Cut - opinion from lived experience
- [ ] Fails the Steven Bartlett test (he would never write this)
- [ ] Could NOT have been generated by prompting AI with "write a LinkedIn post for [your niche]"
- [ ] Every key phrase scores 3+ on the Meaning Score
- [ ] One clear CTA at the end
- [ ] No external links in post body
- [ ] Under 3,000 characters. Sweet spot: 1,200-1,800.
